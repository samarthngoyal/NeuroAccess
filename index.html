The Problem

Many websites are hard to read,
hard to focus on, and hard to trust

People can struggle with long paragraphs, busy layouts, moving content, and unclear words. This can make simple tasks feel stressful.

1 in 5
People may struggle with reading text

Crowded text, tight spacing, and hard words can make reading slower and more tiring.

1 in 10
People may struggle to stay focused

Pop-ups, sidebars, autoplay media, and too many choices can pull attention away from the main task.

54%
Many adults prefer simpler language

When websites use school-like or technical language, many people cannot quickly understand what to do next.

"

A website should help people finish a task, not make them decode the page first.

— NeuroAccess design goal

Common problems people face online:

  • ✕Text is too small or packed too tightly
  • ✕Motion or flashing pulls attention away
  • ✕Words are too formal or too technical
  • ✕Page sections feel busy or unpredictable
  • ✕Buttons are too small or not obvious
  • ✕There is no simple way to hear the page
Our Solution

Choose the view that helps you most.
One tool, five clear modes.

Instead of forcing everyone to use the same page, NeuroAccess offers simple reading modes for different needs.

Helpful suggestion

The tool can suggest a mode, so people do not have to guess which setting might help.

Saves your choice

Your reading mode can stay on for the sites you use often.

How It Works

Simple steps, clear results

  1. 1

    Open any page

    Go to the website you want to read.

  2. 2

    Open NeuroAccess

    Click the add-on button and choose the view that feels easiest for you.

  3. 3

    The page becomes easier

    Text, spacing, layout, and language change to match your chosen mode.

  4. 4

    Your settings stay helpful

    If more content appears on the page, NeuroAccess keeps applying your chosen view.

Features

Useful help for reading and focus.
No extra clutter.

These features are designed to lower stress, reduce distraction, and make websites easier to understand.

🅰️

Reading-Friendly Fonts

Use a clearer font and more comfortable spacing for reading.

Distraction Reduction

Hide sidebars, pop-ups, and other parts that pull attention away from the main text.

Plain Language Help

Turn harder wording into simpler, more direct sentences.
